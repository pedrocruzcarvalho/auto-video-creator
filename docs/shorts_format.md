# Extreme Survival Shorts Format

## Default Runtime

- Target length: 35 to 50 seconds.
- Hard minimum: 25 seconds.
- Hard maximum: 58 seconds.
- Ideal pacing: one new visual beat every 1 to 2 seconds, with one generated video clip per planned beat.

## Required Structure

### 0-2 Seconds: Instant Trap

Goal: make the viewer understand the problem before they can swipe.

Formula:

> You are trapped inside [simple physical survival problem].

Examples:

- Your elevator drops into a flooded shaft.
- Your car sinks, and the window will not move.
- The elevator drops into water and starts sinking.
- You slip under frozen lake ice.

Rules:

- No intro greeting.
- No channel name.
- No "imagine if."
- No slow establishing shot.
- First frame must show the trap already moving.

### 2-8 Seconds: The Rule

Goal: define the game.

Formula:

> There is only one rule: [physical survival rule].

Examples:

- Never stand up on broken ice.
- Do not open the elevator doors until the pressure equalizes.
- Spread your weight before the surface cracks again.

Rules:

- One rule only.
- The rule must be visible.
- If a child could not explain the rule after one watch, simplify it.

### 8-25 Seconds: Escalation

Goal: make the threat feel active.

Escalation tools:

- Timer drops.
- Water rises.
- Sand falls.
- Walls close in.
- Lights turn red.
- Floor cracks.
- Wrong choice triggers a visible consequence.
- Cutaway/simulation shows why the rule works.

Rules:

- Escalation should be physical, not abstract.
- Avoid explaining backstory.
- Show consequence before explaining it.

### 25-45 Seconds: Solution Attempt

Goal: reveal the trick or survival logic.

Solution types:

- Notice a hidden pattern.
- Use the rule against the room.
- Pick the option that looks wrong.
- Wait instead of acting.
- Break an assumption.

Rules:

- The solution should feel clever, not random.
- Plant the answer visually before the reveal.
- Do not use magic unless the episode established magic as the rule.

### 45-58 Seconds: Payoff Or Twist

Goal: make the ending replayable and commentable.

Ending types:

- Escape opens into a worse room.
- The viewer picked the wrong door.
- The timer was not counting down to death, but to reset.
- The safest option was the trap.
- The exit appears, but only for one second.

Rules:

- End on a sharp moving image.
- Do not fade out slowly.
- Leave a clean final beat for comments.

## Script Style

Use second person, present tense, short sentences.

Good:

> You fall under thin ice. The hole disappears behind you. The only safe direction is the darkest patch above.

Bad:

> In this fictional scenario, a person finds themselves in a strange mysterious chamber, and the rules of the situation are slowly revealed.

## Title Formulas

- You Wake Up in [Impossible Place]
- What Happens If [Physical Danger]
- How To Survive [Weird Situation]
- This [Object] Has No [Expected Feature]
- You Have [Time] Before [Threat]
- The [Object] Only Opens When [Rule]
- You Must Choose One of [Number] [Objects]
- Do Not [Simple Action]
- The [Sign/Object/Room] Is Lying

Examples:

- What Happens If You Are Trapped Under Ice
- How To Survive a Sinking Elevator
- What Happens If Quicksand Reaches Your Chest
- How To Survive a Freezer Room Lock-In
- What Happens If Your Space Suit Starts Leaking

## On-Screen Text

Use 1 to 4 words at a time.

Good text:

- NO EXIT
- 60 SECONDS
- DON'T MOVE
- WRONG BUTTON
- ONE RULE
- OXYGEN LOW

Avoid:

- Full subtitles.
- Long sentences.
- Tiny labels.
- Fake readable text generated inside AI video.

## Retention Rules

- First moving image by 0.5 seconds.
- First danger sound by 0.5 seconds.
- First rule by 6 seconds.
- First escalation by 10 seconds.
- Major visual change every 1 to 2 seconds.
- Twist begins before 50 seconds.

## Video-Only Rule

Paid Extreme Survival renders must not use still image plates as final footage.

- Keyframes are allowed only as image-to-video inputs.
- Every planned beat must generate a motion clip.
- If a motion clip is missing, the run should fail instead of assembling stale footage.
- Mock/free previews may use placeholder stills for testing only.

## First 5 Seconds Checklist

Before producing a Short, confirm:

- The first frame shows the trap.
- The first sentence starts with "You".
- The threat is visible.
- There is a strong sound hit.
- The viewer can ask, "What would I do?"
