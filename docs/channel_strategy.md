# Exit Scenario Channel Strategy

## Channel Identity

- Channel name: Exit Scenario
- Handle: @ExitScenarioTV
- Tagline: One situation. One rule. One way out.
- Format: fictional almost-impossible survival situation Shorts, later repackaged into compilations.
- Core promise: every episode places the viewer inside one tricky physical survival situation with one clear rule and one satisfying survival move.

## Audience

Primary viewer:

- Watches Shorts for fast, visual, "what would I do?" scenarios.
- Likes simple 3D explainers, almost impossible accidents, weird survival logic, physical traps, and twists.
- Does not want slow horror, deep lore, chapters, or historical nuance.

The channel should feel easy to understand with the sound off, but much better with sound on.

## Niche

Fictional tricky survival scenarios told in second person.

The recurring fantasy is:

> You are dropped into an almost impossible situation. The physics are simple. The danger is moving. There is one way out.

The videos should not claim to be true. This protects the channel from research burden, fake true-crime risk, and factual accuracy problems.

## Content Buckets

### Weird Survival Simulations

- Trapped under ice.
- Sinking elevator.
- Freezer room lock-in.
- Quicksand rising to your chest.
- Space suit air leak.
- Buried in sand.
- Electrified water.
- Room losing oxygen.

### Almost Impossible Accidents

- Elevator drops into a flooded shaft.
- Car sinks with jammed windows.
- Escalator step collapses.
- Glass bridge starts cracking.
- Conveyor belt pulls you toward rollers.
- Swimming pool drain creates suction.

### Impossible Rooms

- Glass box filling with water.
- Pressure chamber.
- Upside-down room.
- Room that shrinks every time you breathe.

### Wrong Door Scenarios

- Elevator with no buttons.
- Hotel hallway that loops forever.
- Train station that should not exist.
- Bunker corridor with numbered doors.
- Exit sign that lies.

### Countdown Survival Tests

- Oxygen runs out.
- Floor opens in 60 seconds.
- Room fills with sand.
- Heat rises one degree per second.
- Ice cracks from below.

### One Rule Survival Worlds

- Do not blink.
- Do not step on red tiles.
- Do not make noise.
- Do not touch the walls.
- Do not look back.

## What The Channel Is Not

- Not historical explainer content.
- Not fake true crime.
- Not "based on a true story" unless verified and intentionally planned.
- Not dark cinematic horror.
- Not gore.
- Not chapter-based long-form storytelling.
- Not complex lore-first sci-fi.

## Banned Topics

Avoid these unless intentionally approved later:

- Real disasters with fictionalized victims.
- Real missing people, murders, kidnappings, school violence, terrorism, or recent tragedies.
- Instructions for real harm, self-harm, crime, or bypassing safety systems.
- Graphic gore, torture, or realistic suffering.
- Deceptive "this happened" framing.

## Monetization Path

Shorts are the testing engine. Long-form compilations are the watch-hour engine.

Production cadence:

- Publish 5 Shorts per week.
- Every 10 to 15 Shorts, compile the strongest concepts into one 8 to 12 minute long-form video.
- Add a short original intro and outro to compilations so they do not feel like raw reposts.

Targets:

- 30 days: 20 Shorts, one Short above 10,000 views, average cost under $6 per Short.
- 60 days: 40 Shorts, 3 compilations, 100 subscribers, top 2 repeatable formats identified.

## Success Signals

Strong idea:

- Viewers understand the trap in the first 2 seconds.
- Average viewed is 75% or higher on videos under 50 seconds.
- Comments include solutions, guesses, or "part 2" requests.
- Viewers rewatch the twist or rule reveal.

Weak idea:

- Viewers swipe before 3 seconds.
- The rule needs too much explanation.
- The video depends on lore instead of one physical problem.
- Comments say it feels repetitive or generic AI.

## Brand Rules

- Always second person: "You wake up..."
- Always one clear physical rule.
- Always one obvious timer, threat, door, button, gauge, or boundary.
- Always a sound hit on the first visual change.
- Always a payoff, twist, or choice at the end.
