# Extreme Survival Sound Design

## Sound Goal

Sound should make each Short feel expensive, fast, and satisfying.

The channel should not depend on copyrighted trending songs. Build a reusable sound identity instead: fast hits, timers, pressure, doors, alarms, silence, and twist impacts.

## Core Sound Identity

Every Short should use most of these:

- hook sting in the first 0.5 seconds;
- timer beep;
- button click;
- pressure hiss;
- metal door slam;
- bass hit for danger;
- short reverse riser before the twist;
- success unlock sound;
- final glitch or impact.

## Viral Editing Grammar

Use the grammar of viral audio without stealing copyrighted audio.

Patterns:

- Hit on first frame.
- Whoosh on fast visual changes.
- Button click exactly when a button is pressed.
- Bass hit when the room gets worse.
- Timer ticks under countdown sections.
- Half-second silence before the twist.
- Bigger final impact than the mid-video impacts.

## Default Mix

Priority order:

1. Narration.
2. Important SFX.
3. Low ambience.
4. Music, if any.

Rules:

- Narration must always be clear.
- Ambience should be felt, not noticed.
- Do not use continuous loud music under the whole Short.
- Leave tiny moments of silence before major payoffs.

## Sound Sources

Use in this order:

1. Seedance native audio for the first full pass: narrator, motion sounds, water, impacts, and ambience.
2. Burned captions generated from the native audio transcript.
3. YouTube Audio Library or safe royalty-free SFX only if a manual polish pass is needed.
4. Generated SFX or a paid SFX pack later, after the format proves traction.

For now, do not maintain a separate local SFX library. The priority is proving that Seedance can carry the voice and action timing well enough for repeatable Shorts.

## SFX Map

Use this map when planning episodes.

| Story Moment | Sound |
| --- | --- |
| First frame trap | hook sting or bass hit |
| Timer appears | beep + low tick loop |
| Door locks | metal slam + click |
| Button press | dry button click |
| Pressure changes | air hiss |
| Water rises | muffled rush + bubble |
| Sand falls | dry granular pour |
| Room shrinks | low mechanical groan |
| Light turns red | alarm chirp |
| Wrong choice | bass drop |
| Clue reveal | camera click or soft chime |
| Escape opens | unlock + pressure release |
| Twist | silence, then impact |

## Generated SFX Prompt Templates

Use short, generic prompts.

- "short metal door slam, dry, close, no music"
- "single plastic button click, close microphone, no background"
- "fast reverse riser, 0.7 seconds, tension, no melody"
- "deep bass impact hit, short, clean, no music"
- "small digital timer beep, single beep, clean"
- "pressurized air hiss, short burst, industrial"
- "wet water rush inside a small chamber, short"
- "dry sand pouring onto metal floor, close"

## Music Policy

Use music sparingly.

Allowed:

- royalty-free tension beds;
- generated non-melodic pulses;
- very low drones;
- original reusable channel stings.

Avoid:

- copyrighted trending sounds as the core audio;
- recognizable songs;
- full-volume music competing with narration;
- audio that prevents future long-form compilations from monetizing safely.

## Quality Checklist

Before upload:

- First sound happens within 0.5 seconds.
- Every important action has a matching sound.
- Narration is easy to hear on phone speakers.
- Twist has silence or a riser before it.
- Final impact is satisfying.
- No copyrighted audio dependency.
